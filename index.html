First Principles
Thailand's Cinema Industry

Understanding Major Cineplex starts with understanding how the cinema business works in Thailand and why it looks different from Western markets.

🎥
How Cinemas Make Money

A cinema has three revenue engines: ticket sales (box office admissions, ~50% of revenue), concessions (popcorn, drinks — ~26% of revenue but ~43% of profit due to massive margins), and advertising (pre-show ads, screen ads — ~13% of revenue, ~30% of profit). The key insight: concessions and advertising are where the real profit is — tickets are the traffic driver.

🇮🇹
Why Thailand Is Special

Thailand is a virtual duopoly: Major Cineplex (~70%) and SF Corporation (~30%). That's it. Unlike the US with dozens of chains, Thailand's market is locked up. The real twist: Thai films now outperform Hollywood domestically — 62% of box office in 2024 was local content, reversing the historical dominance of Western films. This is a cultural renaissance similar to what India's Bollywood achieved.

📈
Screen Penetration Gap

Thailand has 1 screen per 58,000 people. Compare that to the US (1 per 8,400) or South Korea (1 per 16,000). This massive gap means there's significant room for expansion, especially in Thailand's provincial regions where cinema is a growing urbanization trend. Major is actively targeting 26 new branches in these underserved secondary cities.

🍿
Popcorn Empire

Concessions are the profit powerhouse. Major's branded popcorn is now sold outside cinemas at 14,000+ 7-Eleven stores, on Grab, Foodpanda, and in supermarkets. The new TKN joint venture with Taokaenoi targets 1.5-2.0M bags/month production, scaling from just 0.4-0.5M bags in-house. First-year revenue target: THB 200M.

🎬
M Studio Content

Major owns M Studio — Thailand's #1 film studio with 39% domestic producer market share. They aim to release 20-24 Thai films/year. Vertical integration means they capture margin at both production AND exhibition. Death Whisperer 3 (M Studio) was Thailand's biggest 2025 film — outgrossing Avatar 3.

📱
Digital Ecosystem

M Pass subscription: 200K members at THB 300/month ARPU (targeting 400K). M Generation loyalty: 2M+ active users. Super App with QR ticketless entry. 5G DCP distribution via AIS replacing physical hard drives. Online kiosks replacing human ticket staff to cut wages.

Competitive Advantage
The Major Cineplex Moat

Three interlocking structural advantages make this business extremely difficult to disrupt or replicate.

Scale & Capital Barriers95/100

863 screens vs SF's 395. Economies of scale allow 40-50% revenue-sharing terms with distributors. Capital intensity and prime real estate lock-up make new entry virtually impossible.

Vertical Content Integration85/100

M Studio is Thailand's #1 film producer. Controls supply of Thai films (61% market share in local content distribution). Insulates from Hollywood volatility.

Infrastructure Ownership80/100

Owns many standalone branch buildings (unlike competitors who lease). Full control over screening schedules — earlier first shows, later last shows than competitors. Exclusive IMAX license for Thailand.

Concession Brand Power75/100

Popstar popcorn brand sold across 14,000+ retail locations. JV with Taokaenoi for FMCG distribution. Converting cinema brand recognition into packaged consumer goods.

Investment Debate
Bull Case vs Bear Case

Sentiment is sharply divided. Is this a deeply discounted value play with strong capital returns, or a value trap facing structural industry decline?

The Bull Case

Local Content Renaissance

Thai films captured 62% of box office in 2024. M Studio is Thailand's #1 producer. 69 Thai films slated for 2026 (vs 55 in 2025). This insulates from Hollywood volatility and taps growing provincial demand.

Extreme Valuation Discount

7.1x P/E is -2 standard deviations from historical average (19x). Below COVID-era pricing. Global peers average 18.7x. P/B at ~1.0x vs historical 3.0x. P/S at 0.7x — lower than during the GFC.

Aggressive Capital Returns

THB 600M active buyback retiring up to 9.99% of shares. Over 20% cumulative shares cancelled since 2022. 3.4% dividend yield. FCFF yield of 18.9%. Krungsri models 11% target price upside from buyback alone.

Margin Expansion Story

EBITDA margin expanded to 29%. Q4 2025 net profit surged 175% QoQ and beat consensus by 16-37%. Cost controls: automation, returning underutilized spaces, eliminating low-margin promos, optimizing raw material costs.

FMCG Diversification

TKN popcorn JV creates an asset-light, scalable revenue stream outside of cinema. 14,000+ retail touchpoints. THB 200M first-year target. Reduces box office dependence structurally.

Hollywood Slate Strengthening

2026 features Avengers, Spider-Man, Toy Story 5, Dune, Super Mario. Universal extending theatrical windows from 18 to 35 days (45 by 2027). Paramount's $81B WBD acquisition signals Hollywood isn't dead.

The Bear Case

Secular Streaming Disruption

Post-COVID, viewer behavior permanently shifted. Netflix Thailand: 2.1M subscribers, THB 2.1B revenue. Consumers wait for films on streaming rather than paying for theater tickets. Bangkok attendance structurally declining.

Macro Headwinds

High Thai household debt and weak consumer confidence restrict discretionary spending. GDP growth outlook tepid. Advertising clients cutting budgets — ad revenue fell 19% YoY. Cinema is one of the first budget items consumers cut.

Revenue Sensitivity is Extreme

1-3% decline in ticket revenue triggers 5-7% concession drop and 11-13% advertising decline. The high operating leverage that helps on the upside absolutely crushes on the downside.

Hollywood Slate Unpredictability

Captain America: Brave New World and Snow White both massively underperformed. Despite strong titles on paper, actual performance is impossible to predict. Local Thai film success is equally hard to forecast consistently.

Guest Counts May Never Recover

CLSA argues cinema attendance has secularly declined. Revenue peaked at THB 11B in 2019 — still 30%+ below that level. Even with cost discipline, there may be a structural ceiling to recovery.

Tariff Risks

Proposed 100% US tariffs on foreign-produced films could increase licensing costs for Thai operators. US studios might delay international productions or face retaliatory measures.

Risk Framework
Key Risk & Sensitivity Analysis

Understanding the cascading revenue sensitivity is critical. Box office is the kingpin — when tickets fall, everything else falls harder.

Ticket Revenue -1% to -3%
↓ Concessions -5% to -7%

Direct correlation — fewer guests = fewer popcorn buyers

Ticket Revenue -1% to -3%
↓ Advertising -11% to -13%

Advertisers pay for eyeballs — lower attendance = less ad value

Thesis-Breaking Scenario
Permanent Streaming Shift

If audiences permanently migrate to home platforms, high-margin popcorn and ad sales are destroyed

Key Risks

1. Streaming cannibalization: Permanent shift to home viewing reduces theatrical attendance structurally.

2. Macro weakness: High household debt + weak confidence = less discretionary spending on entertainment.

3. Content dependency: Both Hollywood and Thai film success are inherently unpredictable. One bad quarter of films devastates results.

4. Ad revenue structural decline: Corporate ad budgets shifting digital. Cinema advertising is an early cut in downturns.

5. Geopolitical: Thailand-Cambodia tensions, US tariff threats on foreign-produced films.

6. Family governance risk: Poolvaraluck family controls 51%+ after buybacks, concentrated decision-making.

Key Catalysts to Watch

1. 2026 box office data: Q1 2026 is tracking 50%+ better YoY. Monitor weekly admissions to validate recovery.

2. TKN popcorn JV revenue: Watch for THB 200M first-year milestone. Proof of concept for FMCG diversification.

3. Margin sustainability: Can Q4 2025 margins (12.5% operating) be maintained? SG&A-to-sales of 27.3% is a key metric.

4. Share count: Analysts model 760M shares for 2026. Actual will be ~695M. EPS revision when they update = catalyst.

5. Thai film success: 69 Thai films in 2026. Monitor Death Whisperer franchise and other M Studio titles.

6. Hollywood theatrical windows: Universal extending to 35-45 days. Other studios following?
